Graduate Research Assistants

  • Nathan Gibson
    Nathan Gibson is a Ph.D. Candidate in Arabic and Syriac (Semitic Languages: Christian Near East) at The Catholic University of America. His interests are in early Christian-Muslim encounters and the cultural dimension of religious argumentation.
  • Daniel Greeson
    Daniel Greeson is currently a Master of Theological Studies candidate at Vanderbilt Divinity School. He previously earned a Master of Library Science from Indiana University and a Bachelor of Arts in Religious Studies and Philosophy from Western Kentucky University. He is interested in digital humanities, rare books/manuscripts, various human artifacts, and how all these disparate interests intertwine. His research interests, broadly speaking, lie in how modern thinkers retrieve and receive pre-modern philosophical and theological works.
  • Tucker Hannah
    Tucker Hannah is a Master of Theological Studies student at Vanderbilt Divinity School. He graduated from Furman University with a Bachelor of Science degree in Psychology, where he also majored in Religion and minored in Ancient Greek and Roman Studies. He has spent the past two years working on manuscript digitization with the Homer Multitext Project where he first discovered a love for rediscovering ancient manuscripts and making them available to a modern audience. His primary research interests are in the textual traditions of the first four to five centuries of Christianity.
