Using TEI to Catalog Syriac Manuscripts

Syriaca.org publishes its manuscript catalog information in XML, using the TEI (Text Encoding Initiative) Guidelines. This means our data is compatible with many other projects, as well as machine-readable, making powerful searches and collaboration possible.

If you are interested in encoding information about Syriac manuscripts in TEI, the following tutorials are for you. The example used is from vol. 1 of William Wright's Catalogue of Syriac manuscripts in the British museum acquired since the year 1838 , which may be downloaded from archive.org here. You can find the TEI template file used in the tutorials here. The XML editor used in the tutorials is Oxygen XML Editor, but you may use any other XML editor or even a simple text editor. The "Word file" referred to in the videos simply contains the text of Wright's manuscript catalog.

Note: These videos were originally recorded for internal Syriaca.org use and may refer to materials that are only available within the Syriaca.org organization.
