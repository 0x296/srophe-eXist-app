Volume I: Qadishe

Qadishe contains information on persons relevant to the study of Syriac hagiography include the saints themselves (such as St. Simeon the Stylite) as well as their associates and the authors who narrated their lives or wrote homilies and hymns about them. Persons in the database can be annotated with relationships to other people and texts, and link with other Syriaca.org resources, such as the descriptions of places, manuscripts, or bibliography items. Qadishe is organized according to the name of the saint, in English, French, and Syriac. It lists known dates associated with the saint (birth, floruit, death). It also lists relationships between the saint and other ancient persons. Finally, it contains geographical information on locations associated with the saint (birthplace, floruit place, place of death, place of relics, place of veneration). Qadishe takes as its starting point the standard reference on Syriac saints, J.-M. Fiey's Saints syriaques (Princeton, 2004) but also cites other major references including the BHO, GEDSH, and the BSO.

Another volume in this same series, Volume II: Bibliotheca Hagiographica Syriaca Electronica describes texts written about saints and may be found here.
